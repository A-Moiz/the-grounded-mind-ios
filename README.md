# The Grounded Mind

> **Disclaimer:** This project is not intended to attack, demean, or disrespect any individual, faith, or worldview. Its sole purpose is to present evidence, reason, and scripture in an honest and thoughtful way. All are welcome to engage with the content openly and critically.

---

## What is The Grounded Mind?

**Dawah — Sound Reasoning** is an educational app designed to explore questions of faith, philosophy, and worldview through evidence-based reasoning and academic references. Users select their worldview on launch - Atheist, Christian, Muslim, Others - and are presented with topics relevant to their perspective, each supported by peer-reviewed sources, scriptural references, and reasoned argument.

## Contributing

Contributions are welcome. Whether you want to suggest a relevant image, a scriptural verse worth covering, a topic that hasn't been addressed, or a correction to existing content - your input helps make this a more thorough and accurate resource.

Everything described below can be done entirely through the GitHub website — no terminal, no git commands, no technical setup required.

### What you can contribute

- **Images** — Diagrams, historical images, or illustrations relevant to existing or new topics
- **Verses & references** — Scriptural verses, peer-reviewed papers, or academic sources that support or expand on a topic
- **New topic suggestions** — A topic you feel is missing from any of the sections, with an explanation of what it would cover and why it matters
- **Corrections** — If you believe a claim is inaccurate, misrepresented, or missing important context, raise it

### How to contribute

1. **Fork this repository**

   Click the **Fork** button at the top right of this page. This creates your own personal copy of the repo under your GitHub account where you can make changes freely.

2. **Create a new branch on your fork**

   On your forked repo, click the branch dropdown (it will say `main`) → type a new branch name describing your contribution → click **Create branch**. For example:
   ```
   add-fine-tuning-diagram
   suggest-islam-embryology-verse
   fix-beginning-of-universe-image
   ```
   Working on a separate branch keeps your contribution isolated and makes the review process cleaner.

3. **Make your changes**

   On your new branch, make your changes directly through the GitHub website:

   - **To upload an image:** Navigate to any folder → click **Add file** → **Upload files** → drag and drop your image and commit it to your branch. Don't worry if you're unsure which folder to place it in — just upload it and specify where it should go in your PR description (Step 5).
   - **To suggest a verse, topic, or reference:** Click **Add file** → **Create new file** → name it something descriptive (e.g. `suggest-embryology-verse.md`) → write your suggestion in the text box and commit it to your branch.
   - **For a correction:** Navigate to the relevant file → click the pencil ✏️ icon to edit it → make your change → commit it to your branch.

4. **Check your contribution against these standards**

   Before submitting, make sure your contribution meets the following:
   - Verses and references must include the full source — author, title, publication, year, and a URL where available
   - Claims must be specific and argued - "this verse shows X because..." not just "this verse proves X"
   - No content that is intentionally inflammatory, disrespectful, or unsourced

5. **Open a Pull Request**

   Once your changes are ready, go to your forked repo and click **Contribute** → **Open Pull Request**. In the PR description, please include as much detail as possible:

   - **What** you are contributing
   - **Why** it is relevant to the topic or section
   - **Where** it should be used - which topic, which worldview section, and which folder the file belongs in if you weren't able to place it there yourself
   - **References** — any academic, scriptural, or credible sources that support the contribution
   - If suggesting a new topic, include a short outline of what the content would cover and why it belongs in the app

   The more detail you provide, the faster your contribution can be reviewed and merged.
